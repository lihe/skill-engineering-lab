## Title Checklist

Check:

- Does the title sell a visible result rather than only a product name?
- Is there a concrete scene, pain, number, or contrast?
- Can a viewer understand the hook in one glance?
- Does the title avoid the product name by default?
- Does it avoid generic launch wording?
- Does it avoid pure technical parameter stacking?
- Are there 8-12 title candidates when ideating?
- Are at least 4 candidates using different B站 high-hook buckets such as recognition gap, old-action interception, creator-tested proof, or result shock?
- For brand briefs, have unsupported extreme words been softened while keeping the hook?
- Are the top 3 titles marked with brief reasoning?

Red flags:

- "某产品深度测评"
- "某功能全面解析"
- "AI 正在改变未来"
- Brand name is the only hook.
- Product name appears in every title without a clear strategic reason.
- Too many nouns, no result.
- Unverified absolutes such as "最强", "唯一", "全网首发", "吊打", "碾压", or "99%的人不知道".

## Cover Checklist

Check:

- Does the cover text sell the first-glance result rather than summarize the whole video?
- Is it shorter and harder than the title?
- Is it 2-3 lines with one idea per line?
- Are both 2-line and 3-line options provided when ideating?
- For 2-line options, is each line 7-10 Chinese characters?
- For 3-line options, is each line 5-7 Chinese characters?
- Does each candidate vary line lengths when possible, such as 7/9 or 5/6/7?
- Does every line stay within the maximum for its format?
- Does it first borrow a Jack-Cui-inspired title hook before compressing into cover text?
- Does it use at least one high-tension move: old-action interception, plug-in metaphor, role reversal, result shock, or "真能干活" proof?
- Can it be understood in one glance?
- Does it avoid pasting the full title onto the cover?
- Does it avoid using the product name as the only hook?
- Does it avoid hashtags, CTA, download links, and platform notes?
- For brand briefs, have unsupported extreme words been softened?
- Does the recommended cover text pair naturally with the recommended title?

Red flags:

- Full title pasted onto the cover.
- Lines that are too short to carry a hook or too long to read at a glance.
- Every line has the same length, making the cover feel flat.
- Flat feature labels such as "自动写日报 / 到点生成" with no tension, contrast, or curiosity.
- Multiple technical nouns with no visible result.
- More than one hook fighting for attention.
- Product name plus generic words such as "深度测评" or "功能解析".
- Unverified absolutes such as "最强", "唯一", "全网首发", "吊打", "碾压", "最安全", or "99%的人不知道".

## Outline Checklist

Check:

- Is the outline three-part by default?
- Does part 1 show a result or pain before product background?
- Does part 1 end with a brief product intro after the result, pain, or contrast?
- Does that intro include only product name, positioning, and the episode-relevant capability?
- Does part 2 prove product value through real tasks?
- Does part 2 avoid becoming a feature list?
- Does every major feature connect to a demo, scene, or viewer pain?
- Does part 3 elevate from feature to workflow change?
- Is CTA natural and not suddenly inserted?
- Does the outline avoid internal labels such as "画面/demo", "口播重点", or "目标"?
- Does the outline avoid publishing hashtags or platform-operation notes?
- Does the ending include a clear "一句升华"?

Segment targets:

- Part 1: 180-250 spoken characters.
- Part 2: 550-700 spoken characters.
- Part 3: 150-220 spoken characters.

## Demo Checklist

Check:

- Can the demo be explained in one sentence?
- Is there a visible result in the first 10 seconds?
- Does the demo have contrast, humor, or surprise?
- Does it prove the product's core capability?
- Can it be shown on screen clearly?
- Is there a second-round modification or comparison when relevant?
- Does the demo avoid requiring too much technical background?

If the demo is not memorable, redesign before writing the script.

## Script Checklist

Check:

- Does the opening 10 seconds contain a concrete result?
- Does the script start from the result/demo when possible instead of slow background explanation?
- If the first shot is a visible result, does the first line address the viewer directly, such as "你现在看到的这份/这个..."?
- Is the script divided into the same three parts as the outline?
- Does each script part correspond to the matching outline section?
- Does the first section include contrast?
- Does the first section end with a concise product intro rather than begin with it?
- Is that intro short and directly connected to the next test/demo?
- If the video is about creating a Skill, Agent, or workflow, does the part 1 transition promise a process walkthrough rather than only the final task?
- Does part 1 avoid revealing the same product name twice?
- Does the product name appear naturally rather than as hard ad copy?
- Are technical points translated into ordinary scenes?
- Is the middle section an actual test, not just selling points?
- For process walkthroughs, does the script use clear step markers such as "首先", "接下来", and "最后再到"?
- Does iteration use inclusive language such as "我们还可以..." when guiding the viewer through changes?
- Does the walkthrough close the loop before the insight, such as "这样，整个skill流程就制作完毕了"?
- Is there at least one memorable demo?
- Is there a "不是 X，而是 Y" value judgment?
- Does the ending move from product to workflow or creation style?
- Is the CTA natural?
- Does the script sound like creator口播, not brand PR?
- If using B站 high-density口播, does the script quickly move from hook to test instead of staying in exposition?
- Are there viewer-guiding lines such as "可以看到", "接下来", "重点是", or equivalent natural phrasing?
- Does the script borrow rhythm and structure without copying another creator's fixed sign-off or personal identity?

Length:

- Effective spoken characters: 850-1050 by default.
- Script document characters: 1000-1200 by default.
- Final video target: 3:00-3:30.
- CTA should usually stay under 80 characters.
- If there are many visual demos, stay closer to 850-950 spoken characters.
- If the product is infrastructure-heavy, stay closer to 1000-1200 document characters but keep sentences short.
- If the user explicitly wants a denser B站 rhythm, 1050-1200 effective spoken characters is acceptable, but only when the demo can support the speed.

## Language Checklist

Use:

- Short sentences.
- Conversational rhythm.
- Concrete verbs.
- Viewer-guiding lines like "你会发现".
- Value transitions like "真正关键的是".
- Old/new comparison.
- Identity anchors such as 普通人、打工人、程序员, only when they match the brief.
- Test-and-proof phrasing before claims.

Avoid:

- Long technical paragraphs.
- Consecutive feature lists.
- Empty praise.
- Formal press-release tone.
- Ending with only a slogan.
- Unsupported high-click wording such as "99%的人不知道", "最强", "吊打", or "全网首发".

## Golden Sample Comparison

When judging whether a script matches the established style, compare it with `golden-sample-qclaw.md`.

Check:

- Does the first sentence feel like the creator is pointing at a visible result with the viewer?
- Does part 1 follow result -> pain/contrast -> product intro -> process promise?
- Does part 1 reveal the product name only once?
- Does part 2 feel like taking the viewer through a process, not listing features?
- Are step markers and inclusive phrases used naturally?
- Does the script close the process before giving the value judgment?
- Does part 3 elevate from product function to reusable workflow or creation method?

Use the sample for rhythm and structure only. Do not copy QClaw-specific claims into unrelated briefs.

## Technical Translation Checklist

For each technical term, ask:

- What does this change for the user?
- Is it faster, cheaper, more stable, easier, or more controllable?
- Can this be shown through a demo?
- Can it be explained in one sentence?

Examples:

- "长上下文" means "可以一次读完整个项目/文档".
- "Serverless" means "不用自己准备 GPU 或搭环境".
- "存算分离" means "计算不用等数据".
- "PBR 材质" means "不同光照下更接近真实材质".

## Final Output Checklist

When delivering a complete creative package, include:

1. Title candidates.
2. Recommended title.
3. Cover text candidates and recommended cover text.
4. Three-part outline.
5. Demo plan.
6. Final spoken script.
7. Character count and estimated duration.
8. Self-check summary.

If the user only asks for one artifact, generate that artifact and include only the relevant checklist notes.
