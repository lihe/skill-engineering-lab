## Overall Voice

The script should sound like a creator explaining an AI product through a concrete experiment.

It should not sound like:

- A press release.
- A product manual.
- A benchmark report.
- A generic ad read.

It should sound like:

- A fast, clear, curious creator.
- Someone showing a real result first.
- Someone translating technical capability into everyday scenes.
- Someone who can be playful at the start and serious at the end.

## Length And Timing

Default target:

- Final video length: 3:00-3:30.
- Effective spoken Chinese characters: 850-1050.
- Script document length: 1000-1200 characters.

Effective spoken characters exclude:

- Outline.
- Video notes.
- Shot descriptions.
- Media links.
- Title candidates.
- Table labels.

Segment allocation:

1. 产品引出: 180-250 characters.
2. 亮点 + 实测展示: 550-700 characters.
3. 价值总结 + 行动号召: 150-220 characters.

Product-type tendency:

- AI app / agent / coding tool: 950-1100 characters.
- AI video / 3D / voice model: 850-1000 characters.
- Cloud / compute / storage / training framework: 1000-1200 characters.
- Hardware: 950-1150 characters.

High-density B站 mode:

- Use when the user asks for a faster Jack-Cui-like口播 rhythm.
- Public subtitle samples show a much denser rhythm, often around 1100-1500 spoken characters for 3-4.5 minute videos.
- For this skill, borrow the energy and sentence design, not the full density by default.
- Keep normal 3:00-3:30 scripts at 850-1050 effective spoken characters.
- If the brief has many demos and the user wants a denser B站 version, use 1050-1200 effective spoken characters and cut pauses.

Compression rules:

- Opening 30 seconds: 130-160 characters.
- Each core capability: 120-180 characters.
- One technical parameter explanation: max 2 sentences.
- CTA: max 80 characters.

If over 1200 characters, cut in this order:

1. Brand background.
2. Repeated adjectives.
3. Technical explanation without a visible demo.
4. Repeated praise in the ending.

If under 800 characters, add in this order:

1. A more concrete demo process.
2. An old-way vs new-way comparison.
3. A "不是 X，而是 Y" value judgment.
4. A smoother setup before CTA.

## Rhythm

Use short spoken sentences.

Rules:

- One sentence carries one idea.
- Every 3-5 sentences should move the story forward.
- Important conclusions should stand alone.
- Do not pack multiple technical concepts into one long sentence.
- Use line breaks naturally for口播 rhythm.

Preferred rhythm:

1. Fast hook.
2. Clear contrast.
3. Simple product reveal.
4. Task-by-task demo.
5. One strong value judgment.
6. Short CTA.

Opening rule:

- Start directly with the visible result whenever possible.
- When the screen already shows a concrete result, prefer a viewer-facing line such as "你现在看到的这份/这个..." instead of the more distant "这份/这个...".
- Avoid beginning with general pain explanation if a demo/result can appear first.
- Pain can appear after the result as context, not before it.
- The first 10 seconds should feel like "look what happened", not "let me explain the background".
- The first section should usually end with a concise product intro after the hook/result/pain.
- Keep that intro to 1-3 short sentences, roughly 40-90 Chinese characters.
- Mention product name, positioning, and the one capability tied to this video's demo. Do not list the full product.
- Avoid duplicate product reveals in part 1. If the ending uses "这次我用的是 [产品]", earlier hook lines should say "AI", "这个工具", or "它" instead of naming the product.
- When the product is revealed after a visible workflow result, prefer a result-to-tool handoff such as "帮我把这套流程做出来的，是 [产品]" over a generic reveal like "这次我用的是 [产品]".
- Keep the opening personal and concrete. Use "我没有...", "我只是..." when describing the creator's experiment; dropping the subject can make the line feel less spoken.
- Avoid client-facing product phrases in spoken lines. Prefer creator-native language like "替你跑办公流程", "把流程做出来", and "跑起来" over "接入办公场景" or "实现某某能力".
- If a single-task demo can become a long-term workflow, add a compact extension line: "不只是 [单点任务]，它还能继续沉淀成 [长期任务 A]、[长期任务 B]。"

## Jack-Cui Inspired Spoken Layer

Use this layer when the script needs stronger B站技术区口播感. The goal is to borrow the rhythm and structure, not copy any transcript or personal sign-off.

Sample-backed traits:

- High information density: short subtitle lines, usually one small idea per line.
- Hook before product: start with a scene, shock, question, hidden use, or result before naming the product.
- Identity anchor: quickly tell the viewer who should care, such as 普通人、打工人、程序员、手残党、AI 玩家.
- Fast turn into test: after the hook, move into "直接看/实测/跑一下/看结果".
- On-screen guidance: repeatedly orient the viewer with "可以看到", "接下来", "重点是", "最后".
- Old/new compression: explain why the tool matters by contrasting old manual work with the new AI workflow.
- Light emotion, then proof: words like 离谱、夸张、神器、外挂 work only when immediately followed by a visible task or result.
- Fixed creator sign-off is not reusable: do not copy personal sign-offs or creator identity lines.

Opening types:

- Result-first: show the finished thing or strange use case in the first sentence.
- Recognition gap: tell viewers they may have missed a hidden use, trick, or workflow.
- Pain identity: start from a specific person's pain, then reveal the AI way.
- News-to-test: mention a new model/tool briefly, then immediately test what it changes.
- Mini-story: use one compact background line only when it makes the demo more interesting.

Middle structure:

1. State the test task in one sentence.
2. Show the first visible result.
3. Explain the key capability in ordinary language.
4. Add one comparison: old workflow vs AI workflow.
5. Iterate once, such as changing prompt, adding a constraint, or testing a harder case.
6. Land on a practical judgment: who should use it, what it replaces, or where it still has limits.

For process walkthroughs, make the viewer feel accompanied through the steps:

- Start steps with light order markers such as "首先", "接下来", and "最后再到".
- Prefer inclusive action lines such as "我们还可以继续加一个限制" over solitary lines like "我继续加一个限制".
- After the build is complete, explicitly close the loop: "这样，整个 [skill/工作流] 流程就制作完毕了。"
- Then give the insight with "我觉得，这里最有意思的不是 [表面结果]，而是 [流程价值]。"

Line rhythm:

- Prefer 8-16 Chinese characters per spoken line when drafting.
- Use more paragraph breaks than a normal written article.
- Avoid multi-clause sentences. Split them into two or three punchy lines.
- Let important judgments stand alone.

Commercial adaptation:

- Keep the hook, but soften unsupported claims.
- Do not use "99%的人不知道", "最强", "吊打", or "全网首发" unless the brief proves and permits it.
- Replace extreme words with demo-backed language such as "我实测后有点意外", "很多人可能还没注意", or "这次更接近能用".
- For brand briefs, end with a value elevation and natural CTA instead of a personal sign-off.

## Common Sentence Patterns

### Pattern A: Not X, But Y

Use for value elevation.

Templates:

- 它不是 [旧理解]，而是 [新定位]。
- 真正改变的不是 [表面能力]，而是 [底层方式]。
- 它解决的不是 [浅层问题]，而是 [深层问题]。

Use when moving from feature to meaning.

### Pattern B: Old Way vs New Way

Use for efficiency, cost, barrier, and workflow changes.

Templates:

- 以前做 [任务]，你要 [复杂流程]。现在只要 [简单动作]。
- 过去 [旧成本]，现在 [新成本]。
- 原本 [旧门槛]，现在 [新体验]。

Use when the product collapses a workflow.

### Pattern C: You Will Notice

Use to guide viewers through a demo.

Templates:

- 你会发现，它不是...
- 你会发现，真正关键的地方在...
- 你会下意识觉得...

Use when explaining what viewers should observe on screen.

### Pattern D: Key Point Transition

Use before the most important insight.

Templates:

- 最后，也是我觉得最关键的一点...
- 这也是我觉得它最有价值的地方...
- 真正让我意外的是...

Use once or twice only.

### Pattern E: Idea To Reality

Use for AI creation tools.

Templates:

- 只要你脑子里有想法，它就能帮你做出来。
- 从一句需求，到一个能跑的成品。
- 让灵感不再停在脑海里，而是直接落地成应用。

### Pattern F: Test And Proof

Use for B站-style demo pacing.

Templates:

- 我们直接看结果。
- 接下来我用它做一个 [具体任务]。
- 首先打开 [产品]，直接输入一句话。
- 你可以看到，它不是 [表面动作]，而是在 [关键能力]。
- 我们还可以继续加一个限制，看看它还能不能接住。
- 这样，整个 [skill/工作流] 流程就制作完毕了。
- 到这一步，差距就很明显了。

### Pattern G: On-Screen Viewer Address

Use as the first line when the video opens on a visible result. It makes the口播 feel like the creator is talking with the viewer while pointing at the screen.

Templates:

- 你现在看到的这份 [结果]，是 AI [完成方式]。
- 你现在看到的这个 [成品]，不是模板，也不是手动做的。
- 屏幕上这个 [结果]，是我刚刚用 AI 跑出来的。
- 先看结果：这就是 [任务完成后的状态]。

Use this only when the visual result is actually on screen. If the opening is a problem scene rather than a result, use an identity pain opening instead.

### Pattern H: Identity Pain

Use when making the viewer feel the scene is about them.

Templates:

- 对 [人群] 来说，真正麻烦的不是 [表面任务]，而是 [重复动作]。
- 如果你也经常 [痛点]，这个用法会很有感觉。
- 普通人不一定需要学会 [复杂技能]，但一定需要 [结果]。

### Pattern I: Brief Product Reveal

Use at the end of part 1, after the result or pain has already landed.

Use the first template only if the product has not already been named in part 1. If it has, skip the reveal sentence and move directly to positioning.

Templates:

- 这次我用的是 [产品]。
- 帮我把这套流程做出来的，是 [产品]。
- 简单说，它是一个 [定位]。
- 和普通 [旧工具] 不一样，它这次主要负责 [本期任务]。
- 所以接下来，我们直接用它跑一遍 [任务]。
- 如果本期重点是创建 Skill、Agent、工作流或自动化流程，用："接下来，我就带你跑一遍 [Skill/工作流] 制作流程。"

## Tone By Section

### Opening

Tone: light, fast, a little playful.

Useful words:

- 你敢信吗
- 别笑
- 没想到
- 你现在看到的这份/这个
- 这就是我刚做出来的
- 重点是
- 直接看结果
- 很多人可能还没注意
- 我实测后有点意外

Avoid:

- Long brand background.
- "今天给大家介绍..."
- Too many technical terms before the demo.

Product intro rule:

- Put the brief product intro near the end of this section.
- Treat it as a bridge into the test/demo, not as a standalone ad paragraph.
- Say only what the viewer needs to understand the next section.

Opening preference:

- If the first shot is the finished result, use direct viewer address: "你现在看到的这份/这个...".
- This should feel like pointing at the screen with the viewer, not reading a headline aloud.

### Middle

Tone: clear, explanatory, not stiff.

Rules:

- Explain while showing.
- Use metaphors if the product is technical.
- Keep returning to what the viewer can see.
- Do not become a documentation narrator.

Useful lines:

- 接下来我们直接实测一下。
- 首先打开...
- 不用复杂配置。
- 你可以看到...
- 对比一下就很明显。
- 我继续加一个限制。
- 这里最关键的不是...
- 这一步其实是在验证...

### Ending

Tone: more serious, elevated, emotionally grounded.

Rules:

- Summarize product value.
- Move from feature to workflow change.
- Keep CTA natural.

Useful lines:

- 到这一步，其实已经很清楚了。
- 它不只是...
- 如果你也想...
- 也许你缺的不是技术，而是...

Avoid:

- Copying another creator's fixed sign-off.
- Ending with only "本期内容就是这么多".
- Ending before the value judgment has landed.

## Technical Explanation Rules

When explaining technical details:

- Translate every parameter into a human outcome.
- Tie numbers to speed, cost, quality, stability, or accessibility.
- Use one technical term, then immediately explain why it matters.
- Never list more than 3 technical terms in a row.

Examples of translation:

- "256K 上下文" means "整个代码仓库可以一次读进去。"
- "PBR 材质" means "不同光照下更像真实材质。"
- "Serverless 训练" means "不用自己准备 GPU，也能直接云端开训。"
- "存算分离" means "GPU 不用一直等数据。"

## Script Output Format

When writing the final script, output:

```markdown
## 标题
[chosen title]

## 脚本
### 第一部分｜产品引出
[spoken script section 1, matching outline part 1]

### 第二部分｜亮点 + 实测展示
[spoken script section 2, matching outline part 2]

### 第三部分｜价值总结 + 行动号召
[spoken script section 3, matching outline part 3]

## 字数与时长
- 有效口播字数: [number]
- 预计时长: [3:xx]
```

The script must correspond to the outline. Do not add a major content block that does not appear in the outline.

If the user also needs shot notes, keep them separate from the spoken script.
